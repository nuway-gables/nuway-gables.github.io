Three-Phase Treatment System

Phase I

Increase understanding and integration of the effect the addiction has had in all life areas.
  • Gain awareness of deep inner feelings without medicating effects of chemicals.
  • Identify interpersonal relationship issues.
  • Develop ways of setting individual goals.
  • Identify the extent and pervasive nature of dependency/addiction.

Phase II

Explore how identity and personality issues affect recovery needs in all areas.
  • Develop patterns to address interpersonal relationship issues.
  • Rebuild thought and coping patterns to maintain freedom from negativity.
  • Integrate and utilize spirituality tools that assist in maintaining sobriety.
  • Become more aware of self and identity.

Phase III

Establish a commitment to a continuum of care which will strengthen and maintain sobriety and healthy attitudes gained in the recovery process.
  • Actively initiate recovery skills on a daily basis outside of a structured setting via school and/or employment.
